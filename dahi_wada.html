Preparing Dahi Wada(y)

Description

Dahi Wada, a cherished Indian delicacy, features lentil dumplings immersed in creamy yogurt and adorned with an array of spices and chutneys. This delightful dish offers a harmonious blend of flavors and textures, making it a favorite among food enthusiasts. Enjoyed chilled, Dahi Wada serves as a refreshing snack or appetizer, perfect for any occasion.

Dahi Wada holds its origins in the rich culinary heritage of India, particularly in regions like Uttar Pradesh and Rajasthan, where it has been savored for generations. This iconic dish showcases the ingenuity of Indian cuisine, blending lentils, yogurt, and spices to create a harmonious medley of flavors. Its roots trace back centuries, reflecting the diverse cultural influences that have shaped Indian gastronomy over time.

What you need to prepare it?

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 cup moong dal (split yellow lentils)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled
  • 2-3 green chillies
  • Salt to taste
  • Thick yogurt
  • Water
  • Sugar to taste
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Mint coriander chutney
  • Roasted cumin powder
  • Red chilli powder
  • Chaat masala
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • POmegranate seeds (optional- to garnish)

Let's use the ingredients above to prepare our favourite dahi waday

  1. Soak urad dal and moong dal overnight. Drain the water and grind them with ginger, green chilies, and salt to a smooth batter consistency.
  2. Form small dumplings from the batter and deep fry until golden brown. Soak the fried wadas in warm water, then gently squeeze out excess water.
  3. Whisk thick yogurt with water, salt, and sugar to make a smooth mixture.
  4. Immerse the drained wadas in the yogurt mixture until coated well.
  5. Arrange the dahi wadas on a plate, drizzle with tamarind chutney and mint coriander chutney.
  6. Sprinkle roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, and chaat masala on top.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro and pomegranate seeds if desired. Serve chilled and enjoy this delightful snack!
This is usually prepared by Muslims in India during Ramadan. Enjoy the cool and creamy yogurt paired with the flavorful lentil dumplings which provides a refreshing contrast to the often rich and heavy foods consumed during Ramadan.
