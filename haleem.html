How to prepare Haleem for Ramadan

Description

Haleem, a rich and hearty dish originating from the Middle East, is a flavorful and comforting porridge-like stew that boasts a blend of wholesome grains, tender meat, and aromatic spices. This beloved dish has made its mark across various cultures, with each region adding its own unique twist to the recipe.

Commonly prepared during festive occasions and religious celebrations, haleem embodies warmth and togetherness, inviting friends and family to gather around the table to savor its delectable flavors. With its thick and creamy consistency, punctuated by the earthy notes of grains and the succulent essence of meat, haleem stands as a testament to the artistry of slow cooking and the joy of communal dining.

List of Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat grains
  • 1/2 cup barley
  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup lentils (preferably a mix of split chickpeas and red lentils)
  • 500g boneless meat (chicken, beef, or lamb), diced
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon graram masala
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Thinly sliced ginger (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Making of Haleem

  1. Rinse the whole wheat grains, barley, oats, and lentils thoroughly, then soak them in water for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight.
  2. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the thinly sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Incorporate the diced meat into the pot and cook until it's browned on all sides.
  5. Drain the soaked grains and lentils, then add them to the pot along with enough water to cover everything. Stir in the garam masala, ground coriander, ground cumin, ground turmeric, and salt.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, covered, for 4-5 hours, stirring occasionally. You may need to add more water if the mixture becomes too thick.
  7. Once the grains and meat are tender and the mixture has thickened to a porridge-like consistency, remove the pot from the heat.
  8. Serve the haleem hot, garnished with chopped cilantro leaves, thinly sliced ginger, and lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the stew.
Enjoy this comforting dish with naan bread or rice for a satisfying meal.
